Meatballs in tomato sauce

Ingredients:

Prep time: 20 minutes
Cooking time: 1 hour
Serves: 4-6

Sauce:

  • 15ml butter
  • 2 x 410g cans tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 10g basil leaves, chopped
  • 3 sticks celery, chopped
  • 2 medium carrots, grated
  • 125ml red wine
  • 500ml beef stock
  • Sugar to taste
  • 1 x 70g tomato puree
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Meatballs:

  • 1kg lean beef mince
  • 250ml oats, ground
  • 30ml fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 125ml parmesan cheese, grated

Pasta:

  • 3 eggs at room temperature
  • 45ml warm water
  • 15ml olive oil
  • 5ml salt
  • 300g bread flour
  • 150g semolina flour, for dusting

Method

For the sauce:

Heat a pressure cooker on medium heat and add the butter, fry the garlic and onion until golden. Place the remaining ingredients into the pressure cooker, seal and allow to cook approximately 30 minutes. Allow to cool before using a stick blender to blend the sauce until slighly smooth. Keep aside.

For the meatballs:

Preheat oven to 190Â°C. In a mixing bowl, combine all ingredients until well mixed. Using a 25ml measuring spoon, measure out the mixture and roll into balls. Place rolled meatballs onto a greased roasing tray and place into the oven. Once meatballs are cooked (browned), pour the sauce over the balls and allow to warm in the oven.

For the pasta:

Sieve together the dry ingredients in a large bowl. Make a well in the centre and place the eggs in, add salt and olive oil. Gently beat the eggs and mix in the dry ingredients with a fork, incorporating the sides of the flour as you mix in the eggs. Mix the dough into a ball, wrap in cling wrap and place in the fridge for 10 minutes. Slice small portions with a knife and roll pasta through the pasta machine on the thickest setting until the dough is nice and smooth. Roll the pasta through the machine, making it thinner by one setting at a time until the pasta is 2mm thick. Attach the cutting roller onto the pasta machine and cut the pasta sheets. Place on a tray dredgen with semolina. Set aside. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil and cook the pasta for 3 minutes until al dente. Serve the cooked pasta with the meatballs and tomato sauce.

Chocolate pudding (Ouma Kilian)



Ingredients:

Prep time: 5 minutes
Cooking time: 10 minutes
Serves: 4-6
  • 2 x 200g of Cadbury chocolate
  • 1 tin condensed milk
  • 8 Tennis biscuits (Optional)
  • 500ml cream

Method

  1. Melt chocolate in microwave oven.
  2. Add condensed milk and stir quickly.
  3. Add to whipped cream.
  4. Mix well.
  5. Crush Tennis biscuits and put it in the bottom of your bowl.
  6. Pour "mix" on top of biscuits and mix.
