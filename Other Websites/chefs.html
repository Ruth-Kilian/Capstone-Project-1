My Favourite Chefs

Chef Gordan Ramsay

Gordon Ramsay started working in kitchens in the mid-1980s when he was a teenager. He got a job in the kitchen of Harvey's restaurant in London, which was then run by a brilliant, charismatic and famously hot-tempered chef named Marco Pierre White. Ramsay credits White as being one of the people who inspired him to have the highest standards in cooking, and to work as hard as possible.

In 1993, Ramsay opened his first restaurant as chef, with assistance from White; "Aubergine" got its first Michelin star fourteen months later. In 1998, his Restaurant Gordon Ramsay was opened and in 2001 it achieved three Michelin stars; the highest amount possible.

He is a master of the basics. Very few chefs can step back onto prep and do it well. He has taught other Michelin starred chefs their trade. He knows how to work hard and he treats those around him with the respect they deserve. He is a patient mentor, but an impatient non-sufferer of fools.

Chef Martin Kobald

Born in Austria, he began his culinary career and completed his training with outstanding results. He worked his way up from Trainee Chef to Chef de Partie, Sous Chef, and finally, Head of Operations at various Austrian hotels and restaurants.

In 1988, Chef Martin, immigrated to South Africa and was recuited by the Southern Sun. In 1995, he established an Austrian delicatessen, Kobald's Catering. He then purchased the acclaimed 33 High Street Restaurant & Bar, situated in Modderfontein and for five years delighted Gauteng restaurant goers with his traditional Austrian and international fare.

Chef Kobald has been involved with SACA since 1988; having served as Chairman for the Gauteng region between 1993 and 1999. Following this, he was appointed to the Board of Directors, and was responsible for managing the portfolio of overseas promotions.

Among his various roles within industry associations, Chef Martin today inspires and teaches young chefs through his ChefMLK School of Cooking.

His slogan says it all "Cook for Life. Cook for Fun!"

Under his careful management, modern French flair and exquisite African innovation make respectful and compatible culinary companions. First at Ma Passion in Greenside and then at Morningside's Sel et Poivre, the Johannesburg food scene has embraced this signature style.

Chef Jacques Torres

Torres was 15 years old, when he began an apprenticeship at a small pastry shop and completed his apprenticeship requirements in two years, graduating first in his class. In 1980 he began working with two-star Michelin chef Jacques Maximin. During that time, Torres also attended culinary school to earn a Master Pastry Chef degree. He taught pastry courses at a culinary school in Cannes from 1983 to 1986. In 1986, Jacques was awarded the prestigious Meilleur Ouvrier de France medal in Pastry; the youngest pastry chef in history to earn the distinction at 26 years old.

Referred to as Mr. Chocolate Jacques Torres is the master on all things related to this confectionery. He opened New York City's first chocolate museum called "Choco-Story New York, Chocolate Museum and Experience with Jacques Torres". His motto "Real is my promise to you".
